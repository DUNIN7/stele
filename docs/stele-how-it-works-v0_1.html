Why this matters for your app

  • No password database to leak — the single most common cause of account breaches doesn't exist in your system.
  • No email tied to identity — no email-based account takeover, no "forgot password" flow to secure, because there isn't one.
  • Phishing-resistant by construction — a fake copy of your login page can't extract a working credential; the passkey is cryptographically bound to your real site's address.
  • The same technology banks and Google use for passwordless sign-in — you get it by mounting one library, not building a security team's worth of infrastructure.

Making the sign-up and sign-in pages yours

The pages you see in the demo (examples/reference_app/) are a starting point, not a fixed design. You're expected to reskin them completely.

Safe to change — everything visual

Open examples/reference_app/static/index.html. Freely change: all the CSS, all the wording, the overall page layout, your logo, your header and footer.

Must NOT change — a handful of exact names

app.js finds specific elements by an exact internal name (an id attribute) and wires real functionality to them. Keep these exact names somewhere on the page, restyle everything around them however you like:

signup-name, signup-btn, signup-totp, signup-qr, signup-secret, signup-code, signup-finish-btn, signup-codes, recovery-codes, login-btn, login-2fa, login-code, login-totp-btn, login-recovery, login-recovery-btn, account-section, whoami, list-btn, add-passkey-btn, logout-btn, passkey-list, log

Also leave alone: the <script> tags for qrcode.js and app.js, and the contents of app.js itself — that file is the actual cryptographic conversation with the browser, and has nothing to do with appearance.

A concrete example

Before (the demo's plain version):

<button id="signup-btn">Register a passkey</button>

After (rebranded — same button, new look):

<button id="signup-btn" class="brand-primary-btn">
  Create my account →
</button>

Same id, so app.js still finds it and still works. Everything about how it looks and reads is now yours.

If you're working with an AI coding agent

"Restyle the sign-up and sign-in pages in examples/reference_app/static/index.html to match my brand — [describe your colors, logo, tone]. Keep every element's id attribute exactly as it is, and don't modify app.js or qrcode.js at all."

That instruction gives your agent everything it needs to reskin the pages safely. For deeper integration — mounting Stele into your own application rather than restyling the demo — point your agent at AGENTS.md in the repo root.
